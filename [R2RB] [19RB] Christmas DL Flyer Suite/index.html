Sourdough Cob
Meringue Tarts

Golden and crispy on the outside and oozy on the inside. What’s not to like about Bocconcini with the delicious nutty flavours of Dukkah. Trust us, you won’t be able to stop at just one.

Serves: Makes 12 pieces

Ingredients:

  • 1/3 cup plain flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp pepper
  • 3 tbsps. Dukkah
  • 3 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 cups breadcrumbs - made using 1/2
    an Authentic Sourdough Cob
  • 12 Bocconcini balls
  • Vegetable oil to fry
  • 1-2 lemons, cut into wedges

Method:

  1. Make breadcrumbs by tearing bread slices into small pieces.
    Pulse in a food processor until fine crumbs form. For best results with
    this recipe, do not use crusts.
  2. Start with three bowls, in one bowl combine the flour, salt and pepper.
    In the second bowl the eggs, and in the third bowl combine the
    breadcrumbs with the Dukkah. Add more Dukkah if you would like a
    stronger flavour.
  3. Dip each Bocconcini into the flour, then the eggs, then the crumb mix.
    Repeat the egg and crumb steps to get a suitable coating on the balls.
  4. Add enough oil to a large skillet pan to shallow fry. Place on a medium
    heat, wait until the oil is hot, then fry the Bocconcini in small batches
    being careful not to overcrowd the pan. Fry for 2-3 minutes to get
    a light golden colour, but not too long or the cheese will be too oozy.
    A deep fryer works well for this if you have one.
  5. Once done, place the Bocconcini onto a plate lined with paper towel to
    absorb any excess oil as you fry batches.
  6. Transfer to a plate when ready to serve. Serve with chunky lemon wedges.

We've combined sweet mernigue with tangy
lemon curd for an awesome flavour experience.

Ingredients:

  • 6 x Bakers Delight Lemon Tarts
  • 1/3 cup Caster Sugar
  • 2 x Egg whites
  • 1 teaspoon vinegar or lemon juice

Method:

  1. Preheat oven to hot, 220C.
  2. In a medium bowl, beat the egg whites with an electric mixer on medium speed for approximately 1 minute.
  3. Gradually add sugar, one tablespoon at a time, beating until sugar
    dissolves and stiff shiny peaks form for approximately 4-5 minutes. Beat in the vinegar.
  4. Transfer the meringue to a pastry bag fitted with a plain tip.
    Pipe the meringue onto each tart, building up a swirl which completely covers the tops.
  5. Bake tarts in the oven for 4-5 mins until the meringue swirls are golden.
